Core philosophy

The 1% rule

Tiny improvements compound dramatically. You don't need to be twice as good — just slightly better each day.

1% better every day = 37× better in a year

Systems beat goals

Winners and losers have the same goals. What separates them is their daily system. Stop obsessing over outcomes — fix the process.

Identity — the deepest lever

The 4 laws — how to build good habits
Law 1

Make it Obvious

📋
Habit scorecard — write all your daily habits. Label each (+), (–), or (=).
Just awareness starts change
📍
Implementation intention
"I will [habit] at [time] in [place]"
🔗
Habit stacking
"After [current habit], I will [new habit]"
🏠
Environment design — put good cue items in plain sight
Book on pillow = you'll read it
🚫 Break bad habits → Make it invisible (remove the cue)
Law 2

Make it Attractive

🎁
Temptation bundling — pair a "need to" with a "want to"
"Netflix only while on treadmill"
👥
Join the right group — we copy habits of people around us
Go where your goal is the norm
💬
Reframe mindset
"I have to gym" → "I get to gym"
🚫 Break bad habits → Make it unattractive (see the downside clearly)
Law 3

Make it Easy

⚡
Motion ≠ Action — planning isn't doing. Only action produces results.
🔁
Repetitions matter more than time. Frequency builds the habit, not duration.
⏱️
2-Minute Rule — scale any habit down to under 2 minutes to start
"Read 1 page" not "Read 30 min"
🏗️
Reduce friction — lay out gym clothes the night before
Design env. to make good habits easier
🚫 Break bad habits → Make it difficult (add friction)
Law 4

Make it Satisfying

🧠
Immediate reward — your brain needs to feel good NOW, not in 3 months
Treat yourself after a workout
📅
Habit tracker — cross off each day. Don't break the chain.
Visual progress is its own reward
💪
Never miss twice — missing once is an accident. Missing twice is the new habit starting.
Get back on track immediately
🚫 Break bad habits → Make it unsatisfying (accountability partner)
Advanced — the goldilocks rule
😴 Too easy
Boring → quit
→
🎯 Just right
Motivated → flow
→
😰 Too hard
Anxious → quit

Habits stick when the task is just slightly above your current ability. Push slightly beyond your comfort zone — not too far.

The never-miss-twice rule: Missing one day is okay. The real damage is missing twice. Always show up the next day, even for just 2 minutes.
